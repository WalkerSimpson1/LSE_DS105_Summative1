## About the Study

### What is this research?

Dr Jon Cardoso-Silva is one of ten LSE AI and Education Fellows ([fellowship programme](https://info.lse.ac.uk/staff/ESE/AI-Fellowships-call)). As part of this fellowship, he is conducting a study in DS105W to investigate whether, how and when Generative AI tools help or hinder genuine learning in a data science course.

The study builds on earlier work from the [GENIAL project](https://lse-dsi.github.io/genial) (Generative AI as a Catalyst for Learning), which developed a framework for understanding how students engage with AI tools during coursework. The GENIAL framework maps student-AI interactions onto established learning theory to identify patterns that support productive learning.

### What data is involved?

The study draws on three sources of data (irrespective of whether you used AI or not in your assignment):

1. **Git commit histories** from your assignment repositories. These show how your work evolved over time: when code was written, how it changed, and what was revised.
2. **Assessment submissions and grades.** These provide the outcome context for understanding learning processes.
3. **AI chat logs**. If you consent to share these, they allow the research team to examine how you interacted with AI tools during the assignment.

Component 1 covers items 1 and 2. Component 2 covers item 3. You can consent to one without the other if you wish.

### How will my data be analysed?

Your data will be analysed using the [GENIAL framework](https://ssrn.com/abstract=5674422), which maps observable behaviours onto stages of experiential learning. The analysis examines learning processes and patterns across the cohort, not individual performance.

Chat logs and Git histories associated with graded assignments will only be analysed for the purposes of the study after the end of the Winter Term, _after_ all grading for that assignment is finalised. **Your participation has zero influence on your grade.**

The research team may use AI tools (such as Claude) and Python scripting as part of the data analysis process, depending on the methodology required. Your data will not be used to train any AI models.

### Will my information be anonymous?

Your name is required on this form so the research team can link your consent decision to your data during collection. In all research outputs (publications, conference presentations, reports), your identity will be anonymised. No identifiable information will appear in any published work.

You can see examples of how we write about student-AI interactions in our published research:

- Sallai, D. et al. (2024) 'Approach Generative AI Tools Proactively or Risk Bypassing the Learning Process in Higher Education', *LSE Public Policy Review*, 3(3), p. 7. Available at: [https://doi.org/10.31389/lseppr.108](https://doi.org/10.31389/lseppr.108).
- Cardoso Silva, J. et al. (2025) 'Mapping Student-GenAI Interactions onto Experiential Learning: The GENIAL Framework' (preprint). Available at: [https://ssrn.com/abstract=5674422](https://ssrn.com/abstract=5674422).

### Do I have to take part?

Participation is voluntary. Declining has no consequences for your grade, your access to course resources, or your relationship with the teaching team. If you consent now but change your mind later, you can either update this file and push a new commit at any time before data analysis begins, or email the research team to withdraw your consent. You don't need to provide a reason.
